Reflection on MiniProject 6: Digital Video

For this project I used video I had shot of my daughter and her friends when I had a camera at home for a project I was doing for a class a few years back. They were over on a Saturday, and we decided to have some fun while they made chocolate chip cookies. The learning content is how to bake from scratch, which is a skill not known as much as it was in the past. This video could be used in many ways - for a fun project or to support a concept being taught in the classroom. I found this video, in need of editing, and thought it would make the perfect raw video to use for this digital video project.

I downloaded the Movie Maker program to my laptop to edit the video and add visual effects. The background music was an uncopyrighted, impromptu piano piece played by an accompianist for a dance class. I received permission from the accompanist to use the piece, and I credited the accompanist in the movie credits. I used Movie Maker to add the instructions for making the cookies (ingredients, oven temperature, etc.) at the appropriate points in the movie. I added splits between frames so I could incorporate different transitional effects. I edited the film to shorten it from the original footage I took. I also used the slow motion feature on one of the sections I split to apply that effect.
